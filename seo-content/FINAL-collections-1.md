## How to choose your surfboard wall mount
Start with the board you display most. A vertical surfboard rack grips the tail and holds the board on its rail, which is ideal for a shortboard or fish in a tight hallway. A horizontal mount supports the board flat by both rails and suits a longboard or a wooden vintage board you want to frame like art. The cork pads matter here: oak is dense and rigid enough to hold a 7–12 kg longboard with zero flex, while the cork distributes the load so the rails never take a hard point. Most of our boards stay on the mount with the fins left in — handy for a thruster setup you ride often — but you can also lay the board fins-up if you prefer.

## Why mount a surfboard on the wall
A board left on the floor or leaned against a wall collects dings, bent fins and yellowing from the sun, and the wax stains whatever it touches. A wall rack keeps the deck untouched and the board off the ground, ready for the next session. It also frees real floor space in a garage or apartment. Beyond the practical side, a board on oak reads as a sculptural piece, not storage — the reason riders pick our racks for a living room rather than a shed.

**Does it hold a longboard as well as a shortboard?** Yes. The mount takes every width and shape, from a 5'8" shortboard to a 9'0"+ longboard, vertically or horizontally.

**Can I leave the fins on?** Yes. The board sits clear of the wall, so a thruster or quad setup stays mounted between sessions.

**Which wall can I fix it to?** Each mount ships with a complete fixing kit — screws, anchors and an Allen key — with anchors for drywall, wood, concrete and stone.
