## How to choose your bike wall mount
Start with weight — it is the first spec that matters. A carbon road bike sits around 7–9 kg, a gravel bike 8–11 kg, and an e-bike can reach 25 kg with the battery in. If your e-MTB is heavy, remove the battery before hanging and always read the stated max load. Solid oak is dense and rigid, so it carries road and gravel bikes with no flex.

Measure your handlebar before you mount in a narrow hallway. Check too for fenders, cables or a rear rack on a commuter — they can sit in the cradle's path.

Finally, choose your orientation. A horizontal bike wall mount holds the bike by the top tube, parallel to the wall, for the most decorative look. A vertical mount hangs it by the front wheel, perpendicular, for maximum floor space. Carbon frames decide it for many riders: ours cradles the tube and never clamps it.
