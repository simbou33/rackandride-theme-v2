# Skateboard — Technical lexicon (English)

## Anatomy
- **Deck**: 7-ply **Canadian maple** (credibility marker); width in inches (7.75"–8.5"+); **nose**, **tail**, **concave**, **kicks**
- **Grip tape** (top); the artwork/graphic is underneath — the side you display
- **Trucks**: baseplate, hanger, **kingpin**, **bushings**
- **Wheels**: urethane; diameter in mm (50–60 mm street, 60–75+ mm cruising); hardness in **durometer** (99A hard, 78A soft)
- **Bearings** (ABEC rating); **hardware** (8 mounting bolts); risers/pads
- **Pop**: a deck's snap; a deck that loses its pop is "dead" — old decks become wall art

## Board types
- **Street/popsicle skateboard**: ~31–32", 6.5–9 lbs (3–4 kg) — tricks, skatepark
- **Cruiser**: 25–32", soft wheels — getting around town
- **Longboard (skate)**: 33–60" — downhill, carving, dancing, freeride; 9–13 lbs
- **Mini cruiser** (penny-style): 22–27", plastic
- **Old-school / shaped decks**: retro shapes, pool — collector pieces
- **Surfskate**: pivoting front truck, surf-style carving — perfect bridge between both worlds

## Skater vocabulary
- Street, park, bowl, vert, ramp; tricks: ollie, kickflip, grind, slide, manual
- **Complete** (assembled board) vs deck only; **setup**; skate shop
- Skaters keep old decks: limited editions, artist collabs → huge SEO angle "**skateboard deck display**" / deck as wall art
- Skater, rider

## Wall mount specifics
- Two display logics: **horizontal** (complete board, ready to ride) and **flat against the wall** (deck displayed like artwork, graphic facing out)
- Complete board: 3–4 kg; longboard: 4–6 kg
- Wheels can mark the wall; grip shouldn't rub → mounts gripping the trucks or deck; "floating" / invisible mounts are a strong selling point

## Natural SEO keywords (EN)
skateboard wall mount, skateboard wall rack, skateboard deck display, deck wall mount, hang a skateboard on the wall, skateboard hanger, longboard wall mount, floating skateboard display, skateboard storage, skateboard room decor, cruiser wall rack.

## Pitfalls
- "Longboard" alone is ambiguous: specify skate or surf.
- No fins, bindings or handlebars in a skate context.
