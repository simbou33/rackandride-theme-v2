# Ski — Technical lexicon (English)

## Anatomy
- **Tips** (front, curved up), **tails** (back), **waist** (center; **waist width** in mm defines the category: ~70 mm piste, 90–110+ mm powder)
- **Edges** (metal; need tuning/sharpening and **rust** in damp storage — key rack argument), **bases** (need **waxing**)
- **Camber** / **rocker**; sidecut and **turn radius**
- **Bindings**: toe piece and heel piece, **DIN setting**; **ski brakes** (often used to clip a pair together — relevant to rack design)
- **Poles** (with baskets and straps — usually stored with the skis); **ski boots** (flex rating); **climbing skins** for touring

## Ski types (per pair, with bindings)
- **Piste / carving skis**: 150–180 cm, 11–15 lbs (5–7 kg)
- **All-mountain skis**: 160–185 cm — most common
- **Freeride / powder skis**: 175–195 cm, wide waist
- **Freestyle / twin-tip skis**: park and rails
- **Touring / backcountry skis**: light (4–6 kg), tech bindings, skins
- **Cross-country (XC) skis**: long, thin, very light
- **Vintage wooden skis**: classic chalet / cabin wall decor
- Skis are measured in **centimeters** and stored **as a pair**, often base-to-base with a ski strap

## Storage context (arguments)
- Seasonal sport: skis sit in storage 8 months a year (garage, basement, **ski room**)
- Bad storage risks: rusty edges, damaged bases, pressure on the camber, de-tuned bindings
- Good practice: dry spot, summer storage wax, no load on the camber
- A family = several pairs + poles + boots → "**multi-pair ski rack**" is a strong keyword
- Chalet aesthetic: crossed vintage skis on the wall

## Skier vocabulary
- Skier; rider (freeride/freestyle); carving, powder (pow), groomers, corduroy, backcountry, ski resort, lift pass, ski season, après-ski

## Pitfalls
- Ski bindings ≠ snowboard bindings: no straps, highbacks or stance for skis.
- Skis come as a pair + poles: a rack that holds the poles is a real plus — say it.
