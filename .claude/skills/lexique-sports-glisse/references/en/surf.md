# Surf — Technical lexicon (English)

## Surfboard anatomy
- **Nose** (front), **tail** (back: squash, round, pin, swallow, fish tail)
- **Rails** (side edges), **deck** (top, where wax goes), **bottom** (hull)
- **Rocker** (nose-to-tail curve), **stringer** (central wood strip)
- **Fins**: single fin, twin, **thruster** (3 fins, most common), quad. Fin boxes: **FCS, FCS II, Futures**. Removable on most boards.
- **Leash** + leash plug; **traction pad / tail pad**; **wax** (melts in heat, stains — strong argument for racks that don't touch the deck)
- **Volume** in liters; **shape** / **shaper**; **glassing** (fiberglass + polyester or epoxy resin)

## Board types (length / weight — key data for a wall rack)
- **Shortboard**: 5'8"–6'6", 5.5–8 lbs (2.5–3.5 kg) — performance
- **Fish**: 5'4"–6'2", wide, swallow tail — small waves
- **Hybrid / mid-length / egg**: 6'6"–8'0" — versatile
- **Funboard / mini-mal**: 7'0"–8'0" — beginners, progression
- **Longboard**: 9'0"–10'0"+, 15–26 lbs (7–12 kg) — classic glide, noseriding
- **Gun**: big-wave board; **soft-top / foamie**: foam boards, surf schools
- **Wooden / vintage boards**: collector pieces, often wall-displayed
- Surfboards are measured in **feet and inches** (6'2")

## Materials & fragility (storage arguments)
- **PU/polyester**: classic, prone to **dings** and pressure dents on the rails
- **EPS/epoxy**: lighter, sensitive to heat (yellowing, delamination)
- Boards left on the floor or leaning on a wall risk dings, bent fins, falls, sun yellowing → a wall rack protects rails and fins

## Surfer vocabulary
- **Quiver** (a surfer's collection of boards — gold keyword: "store your quiver")
- Session, spot, line-up, take-off, barrel/tube, bottom turn, cutback
- **Wetsuit**, **boardbag**; surfer, longboarder; beginner / intermediate / advanced

## Pitfalls
- Disambiguate **longboard** (surf) vs **longboard** (skateboard) in every text.
- Mention whether the rack works with **fins on or off** — credibility detail.
- Never use trucks, wheels, bindings, handlebars in a surf context.
