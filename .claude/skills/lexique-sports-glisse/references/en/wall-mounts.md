# Wall mounts — Cross-sport vocabulary (English) — read for EVERY product page

## Product terms & SEO synonyms (vary them)
**Wall mount** (pivot term), **wall rack**, **rack**, **hanger**, **hook**, **wall bracket**, **display / display rack** (collector angle), **storage rack**, **holder**, wall-mounted + [equipment].

## Two purchase intents to address
1. **Storage / space saving**: garage, basement, hallway, apartment — vocabulary: organize, free up floor space, protect, off-season, space-saving, declutter
2. **Display / decor**: living room, bedroom, office, shop — vocabulary: display, showcase, wall art, minimalist design, floating / invisible mount, gallery style

## Specs to always state correctly
- **Max load (weight capacity)** in kg AND lbs — consistent with real equipment weight (see sport files)
- **Materials**: powder-coated steel, stainless steel, aluminum, **solid wood** (oak, walnut, bamboo, birch plywood); **padding** at contact points: felt, EVA foam, silicone, rubber
- **Installation**: drilling, wall anchors per wall type (**drywall** with proper anchors, brick, concrete, wood studs); hardware included or not
- **Orientation**: horizontal / vertical / angled; capacity: single board, multi-board (quiver), multi-pair, family rack
- Indoor / outdoor (rust-proof coating); **clearance from the wall** (bindings, fins, handlebars)

## Universal arguments
Protect your gear (dings, damp, floor, sun) • free up floor space • keep gear ready and visible • showcase a board/bike you love • **gift idea** for surfers / skaters / cyclists / riders (seasonal keyword: Christmas gift, birthday)

## Cross-sport pitfalls
- Never state a max load inconsistent with the sport (a 5 kg mount can't hold a 25 kg e-bike or a 10 kg longboard).
- "Will it work on drywall?" is the real customer question — answering it = reassurance.
- Always disambiguate **longboard** (surf vs skate) and **bindings** (snowboard part) vs mount/bracket.
