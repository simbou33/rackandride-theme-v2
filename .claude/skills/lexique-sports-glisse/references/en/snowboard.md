# Snowboard — Technical lexicon (English)

## Anatomy
- **Nose**, **tail**; **edges** (rust in damp storage), **base** (extruded or **sintered**, needs waxing)
- **Camber / rocker / hybrid profiles** (flat, camrock); **flex** (soft = freestyle, stiff = freeride)
- **Shape**: **true twin** (symmetrical, freestyle), **directional** (freeride), directional twin
- **Bindings**: baseplate, **highback**, **straps** (ankle + toe), mounted on the board's **inserts** — bindings usually STAY mounted (the rack must accommodate them: thickness, highback)
- **Stance**: width and angles; **regular** (left foot forward) / **goofy** (right foot forward) — describes the rider, not the board
- **Boots**: soft boots with **Boa**, laces or speed lacing; **stomp pad**

## Board types (with bindings)
- **Freestyle / park board**: 140–155 cm, true twin, 9–13 lbs (4–6 kg)
- **All-mountain board**: 150–162 cm — most common
- **Freeride board**: 158–170 cm, directional
- **Powder board**: swallow tail and special shapes
- **Splitboard**: splits in two for backcountry touring
- **Vintage / collector boards**: topsheet graphics displayed as wall art
- Measured in **centimeters** ("a 157")

## Storage context (arguments)
- Seasonal: 8 months of off-season storage
- Stored **with bindings mounted** → wall mount must handle binding/highback depth — a detail that instantly makes copy credible
- **Horizontal** display (topsheet graphic visible, deck as art) or **vertical** (space-saving, garage)
- Risks on the floor: rusty edges, scratched base, board sliding and falling

## Rider vocabulary
- Snowboarder, rider; riding, session; carving, ollie, grab, 180/360, butter, jib
- Powder (pow), park, halfpipe, backcountry, freeride
- **Board culture / boardsports**: same customer often owns surf + snow + skate — useful for cross-sport copy

## Natural SEO keywords (EN)
snowboard wall mount, snowboard wall rack, snowboard display, hang a snowboard on the wall, snowboard hanger, snowboard storage, horizontal / vertical snowboard rack, snowboard wall decor, garage snowboard storage, ski and snowboard rack.

## Pitfalls
- No poles, no "pair" — one board.
- Bindings stay on (unlike surf fins): the rack and the copy must account for it.
